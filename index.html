Who Needs an All-On-6 Dental Implant?

The All-On-6 dental implant treatment is suggested for patients who have a full arch of missing teeth, have partially missing teeth, or who want to get a full arch dental implant by replacing existing teeth.

All-On-6 dental implants are strategically placed at intervals throughout your lower or upper jaw. They must receive sufficient support from the surrounding bone. If you have bone loss, a full set of dental implants is still obtainable using bone grafting techniques.

Benefits of All-On-6 Dental Implants

The All-On-6 dental implant process takes less time than other types of implant procedures and rarely requires bone grafting. They not only look like natural teeth but they function like them too. Also, All-On-6 patients do not experience gum tissue irritation frequently caused by dentures.

Here are some more benefits of All-On-6 dental implants:

  • Swift recovery time.
  • Improves oral health.
  • Enjoy eating and speaking again.
  • Simple home maintenance and cleaning.
  • Pearly white smile.
  • Fixed dentures.
  • Delivers long-term results.

  • Recovery After All-On-6 Dental Implant Procedure

    It is common to feel some tenderness after receiving All-On-6 dental implants, as the mouth will be healing during the first three months of the procedure. Patients may need to use an antimicrobial mouth rinse or take antibiotics for the first week after surgery, and patients should be cautious when eating chewy or hard foods.

    A removable overdenture or dental bridge is seated on the implants after they are fused into the jawline.

    Maintaining proper oral hygiene is crucial after receiving All-On-6 dental implants. Implant failure is generally caused by bacteria and plaque. Patients must keep new teeth clean to ensure the longevity of the implants.

  • How Do All-On-6 Dental Implants Work?

    The All-On-6 dental implant surgery creates a permanent prosthesis by using six implants in the upper or lower jawbone, which act as an anchor for a dental bridge or over-denture. After conducting a comprehensive examination and taking scans and x-rays, your dentist will evaluate the structure of your mouth, assess bone loss, and create impressions to generate a new smile for you!

    Over time, the implants that are positioned in the jaw will fuse to the jawbone. These mimic the natural roots and give you a more natural smile that lasts.
